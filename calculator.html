Wayfinder


  1. Select your Wayfinder from the list in the drop down box.
  2. Choose your level (1-30 are valid)
  3. Choose how many times your Wayfinder has awakened
  4. Choose your affinities (located in the Affintiies tab, these effect weapon and accessory stats)

Your base stats are independent of level. Your internal stats are what the game uses for calculations. Your display stats are what is displayed to the player. See the FAQ for more details.

Note: you cannot normally see the display stats for your just your Wayfinder in-game, as the in-game Character menu combines your Wayfinder's stats with other items.

Frequently Asked Questions

This is a tool for calculating builds in Wayfinder. If you're having trouble using the tool, see the 'Help' buttons in each section on what to do.

A build consists of a Wayfinder (character), a weapon, several accessories, and a variety of echoes. These things contribute to the 9 main stats, displayed to you in the ‘Total’ section. If you want a clear picture of how the stats actually work, see “How do stats actually work in-game?”.

  • Max Health: Determines the amount of health your Wayfinder has
  • Resliience: Determines the amount of resilience your Wayfinder has (the red bar above your health bar)
  • Weapon Power: Increases the amount of damage you deal with your weapons attacks
  • Ability Power: Increase the efficacy of Wayfinder and weapon abilities.
  • Crit Rating: Determines your percent chance to critically hit with weapons and damaging abilities
  • Crit Power: Determines the amount of bonus damage dealt when you score a critical hit
  • Break Power: Determines how much damage you deal to enemy reslience bars
  • Phys Defense: Determines how much phsycial damage you reduce
  • Mag Defense: Determines how much magical damage you reduce

Stats in this game are displayed to you much differently than how they are used internally. The stats you see in-game are actually just a calculation of base stats and internal modifiers.

Your stats start off as base stats which never change. When a Wayfinder, weapon, or accessory levels up, it's power grows linearly. However, the stats it obtains through growth is distributed according to the distribution of the base stats, creating a power budget. All Wayfinders happend to have the same total power budget, and so all Wayfinders are technically equal in terms of stats.

After calculating your stats with the power budget in mind and weighting them according to the budget modifiers, we get internal stats. These internal stats are what the game most likely uses for calculations, like damage calculations, stats gained by various affinities, and applying buffs/debuffs. Then, the internal stats are mulitplied by their display modifiers to get your display stats. These are what the game actually shows to you.

The reason they go through all of this trouble can only be guessed at, but here's my best theory. Base stats are used because having an actual power budget to base all characters off of may be useful for determining balance. Internal stats are necessary because some items are weighted using the budget modifiers differently. Stats growing faster/slower than others due to budget modifiers creates stat complexity. Finally, the game uses display stat modifiers I assume because they want to round the stats to display to the player while keeping decimals somewhat intact. All of this though makes it very confusing for people just trying to min/max, so I made this tool.

TL;DR: Stats start as base stats, become internal stats, and are displayed as display stats. It's complicated.

Wayfinders can awaken after hitting the level cap. This acts pretty much purely as another level, because every Wayfinder in the game is programmed to have the same awakened growth as leveled growth.

Weapons can temper every five levels, and reinforce after they hit thee level cap. Each one acts similar to a level up, though the amount the weapon grows by is usually less than a level up for tempering and more than a level up for reinforcing.

Accessories are found at a static power level that is determined by the conditions you were in when you found it. If you're in a low level dungeon you might find a low level accessory. If you run that same dungeon at a higher tier of difficulty, you might find the same accessory but at a higher level. This is the only way to get higher level accessories, and echoes are the same way. Accessories that are crafted can only be crafted at the level they're offered.

The power levels that show on your Wayfinder and weapons grow linearly, but may be added on to when displayed to the player to show buffs/debuffs, affinities, and other sources of stats. For example, a level 1 Wayfinder has a power level of 120, which will be displayed to the user. If that same Wayfinder equips an echo, that power level will be instead displayed as a combination of the echoes and the Wayfinders power levels. Despite the increase in power level, your base stats and internal stats don't shift to compensate. The same goes for affinities and other buffs. These affinities and buffs increase your stats, and also increase the displayed power level of whatever is being buffed by those effects.

This might not seem weird, but your power level can be forcibly scaled down by the game in some circumstances which also scales down your stats, despite the fact that increasing your power level doesn't also grow your stats. See "Why do my stats change sometimes between areas?" for more information on that.

At the moment, this tool doesn't accurately represent power levels.

Affinitieez nutz.

Affinities are a percentage increase to weapons and accessories. There are three categories for affinities, instinct, discipline, and focus. Your weapon and accessories all have stats that belong to one of the three categories; weapons usually have 6 stats, 2 for each category, while accessories can have 1-3 stats in any category. The Affinities tab is separated into Wayfinder Affinities at the top and Weapon Affinities at the bottom.

Character affinities allow you to spend resources in a category to get affinity in that category. To put it simply, if you have 15% affinity in instinct, all of your instinct stats on your weapons and accessories get 15% better. Additionally, 5% in a single category gives an affinity perk, unique to each character. Weapon affinities are the same as character affinities, except they only apply to your weapon, not to your accessory, and they do not give unique perks.

If you have any character affinities on the character you currently have equipped, the game will automatically update the stats of weapons and accessories in menus to match what the stats they would have on that equipped character. For example, let's say character 1 with 0 affinities in focus looks at a weapon with 100 Weapon Power in the focus category. Character 1 sees the stat for what it really is. If character 2 with 15% affinity in focus looks at the same weapon, they'll see the Weapon Power stat as 115 instead of 100. Be aware of this while window shopping for weapons, as you'll see stats that aren't necessarily representative of that weapon's actual starter stats.

If the game detects that your power level is above the current area's local recommended power level (shown to you at the start of queuing up for an instance), the game will forcibly scale down your power level to match that area's power level + some number. Your stats are also scaled down to compensate to your new power level. How the math exactly works out to do this is unkonwn at the moment. Note that your power level will never scale up to match an area's recommended power level either.

Critical hits in the game, similar to the rest of the stats in this game, aren't very simple.

The percentage at which you critically hit an enemy depends on both your crit rating and the enemies level compared to yours. The more crit rating you have, the more often you'll crit against enemies of higher level. With a high enough crit rating you'll almost always crit against enemies significantly lower level than you. Conversely, a low crit rating means you are unlikely to critically hit enemies near/higher than your level, and may crit sometimes against lower level enemies. Crit power is just that stat responsible for how much damage you do when you crit. This bonus damage is likely added as flat damage rather than multiplicative damage on top of your normal hits.

As for how to go about building, we don't know yet the formula for critical hits so an exact answer is unknown. Generally you can expect to critically hit somewhat often against enemies of similar level to you if you focus on building crit rating at a steady pace. At a certain point Crit Rating caps out, which makes it more beneficial to stack Crit Power.
